GROUP 20: THE BIG DOGS

Agriculture in Tanzania

Data-Driven Solutions for Smallholder Resilience

Andrei Radu Robin Anthonissen Ward Van Hemel Ylias Roggeman Yorben Nevelsteen

A Growing Crisis

Food insecurity is rising in Africa. Unpredictable climates, alternating between severe droughts and flash floods, severely impact agricultural output year over year.

Climate Extremes

Traditional farming methods lack resilience against volatile weather.

Soil Degradation

Farmers face declining soil quality without testing tools.

The POV

"As a farmer, I want to understand my soil quality and know how climate will affect my crops, so I can adapt, rotate crops effectively, and secure my income and food supply."

The Data Gap

High-tech data exists, but it doesn't reach the people who need it most.

Available Data

Satellites and observatories gather massive amounts of soil & weather data.

Farmer's Reality

Steven the farmer has a basic phone, no smartphone, and unreliable internet.

Our Solution

Turning Data into Actionable Farming Wisdom

1. IoT Monitoring

Custom sensor nodes deployed in the field to track real-time soil moisture and health data.

2. Local AI Analysis

FastAPI backend using LLMs and RAG to process data and determine the optimal crop rotation.

3. Direct SMS

Actionable advice is sent directly to Steven's basic handset. No internet required.
